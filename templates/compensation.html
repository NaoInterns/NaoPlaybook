Paychecks

You will receive your paycheck twice monthly as compensation for the previous pay period. For payroll and overtime purposes the workweek starts on Monday and ends on Sunday. If the normal payday falls on a weekend or holiday, you may be paid the day before or the day after the weekend or holiday depending on payroll processing. Direct deposit will not be used without employee consent. Payroll advances will not be made under any circumstances.

Employees may not be compelled to accept wages by direct deposit or debit card. In order to receive wages by direct deposit, employees must provide their signed written consent through Paycom. Providers will be compensated by means of physical check mailed every payday.

You will be provided a W-2 form at the end of the year. Employers are not required and do not check the validity or accuracy of employee withholdings and exemptions as reported on W-4 forms or state equivalents. Employees are solely responsible for reviewing their withholdings each pay period and for updating their W-4 and equivalent state forms as necessary. It may take 2-4 weeks to process any revisions to tax withholdings.

In the event of termination for any reason, final paychecks will be paid no later than the next regular payday.

As with any other policy, paycheck policies may be changed at any time. All employees are encouraged to deal with management directly regarding any concern about wages, hours, or other terms and conditions of employment. However, nothing in this handbook is intended to restrict non-supervisory employees from exercising their right to communicate with fellow employees about such matters, and Nao Medical prohibits retaliation against any employee for the exercise of such rights.

Paycheck Errors

All employees are asked to review your paycheck carefully for errors. If you find a mistake, report it to Human Resources as soon as possible, but no later than thirty (30) days after receiving your check, so that we may take the steps necessary to correct the error. Errors in payroll will be corrected and paid in the next scheduled paycheck after we receive notice. After thirty (30) days, we will assume there are no errors, and your recourse may be limited or unavailable.

Corrections for overpayments will be made within eight (8) weeks. Employees will be given three (3) days written notice prior to deduction to recover overpayment of wages. Recovery for overpayment of wages may be made through payroll deduction without written authorization from employees, as long as such overpayment does not exceed the net wages earned in the immediately subsequent paycheck.

Should the overpayment exceed the net wages in the next paycheck, no more than 12.5% of gross wages will be deducted from each subsequent paycheck, until such overpayment is repaid in full, and no deduction shall reduce the effective hourly wage below the state minimum wage. Employees may dispute overpayment recovery by submitting a written explanation to management.

Garnishment

Nao Medical is required to comply with any court ordered garnishment of your wages. If you are hired by Nao Medical and currently subject to such a court order, you must supply the appropriate documentation and nature of the action immediately. If Nao Medical must garnish your wages, you may be charged a fee for administrative expenses in the amount permitted by state law.

Exempt Employees

It is our policy to comply with FLSA salary-basis requirements, and therefore Nao Medical prohibits improper deductions from salaries of exempt employees. Improper deductions include those for less than a full day of work and for absences due to sickness or injury, except that deductions for sickness or injury related absences can be made from accrued paid sick or vacation benefits, if available. When any such benefits have been exhausted, the weekly salary may be prorated in full day increments for absences of a full day or more. Deductions may be made for absences of a full day or more due to personal reasons unrelated to sickness or injury, for unpaid disciplinary suspensions, and to prorate for the first and last week of work. If the office is closed due to a holiday or other Nao Medical needs that are not the fault of the employee, no vacation, sick or salary deduction will be made.

Overtime

At times, you may be requested to work overtime in order to meet the needs of Nao Medical. However, working overtime is generally discouraged. Exempt employees are not eligible for overtime pay. Non-exempt employees will be paid at their regular rate plus one-half for hours actually worked in excess of 40 hours in a work week, or as otherwise prescribed by state law. Paid time off does not count towards overtime.

Non-exempt employees whose regular rate of pay is at or just above minimum wage will also receive an additional hour of pay at minimum wage if they work a split shift or work more than 10 hours in a workday (including break times).

Nao Medical complies with New York City's Commuter Benefits Law and provides the option for employees who have worked an average of thirty (30) hours or more per week for a minimum of four (4) weeks to participate in plan that will allow them to utilize pre-tax money to cover certain types of qualified transit expenses. Health Benefits

In accordance with the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (PPACA), Nao Medical offers all employees who work more than thirty (30) hours per week (or 130 hours per month) access to affordable health insurance coverage for themselves and their dependents up to age twenty-six (26). Employees become eligible to participate on the first day of the month following sixty (60) days of continuous employment, and no later than ninety (90) days. Spouses may be added to coverage at the employee‚Äôs sole expense. Employees should consult the Plan Documents for more details of the plan and any Nao Medical provided contributions. Where the Plan Documents and this policy conflict, the Plan Documents control. Nao Medical contributions may be changed at Nao Medical‚Äôs sole discretion.

Disability Benefits

Nao Medical provides state required disability insurance benefits for employees who have a non- work-related injury or illness, or a disability due to pregnancy, beginning with the 8th consecutive day of disability. This plan includes Paid Family Leave coverage, as required by law and as outlined in the New York Paid Family Leave policy.

Please see management for additional information and claim forms. This is a cash wage replacement benefit paid directly through the insurance carrier; medical bills are not covered.

Employees whose regular work schedule never achieves twenty-six (26) weeks or 175 days in a 52-week period have the option to waive this coverage. Please see the management if you wish to do so.

Employee Medical Benefit

Nao Medical has an in-house medical treatment plan that allows full-time employees who have successfully completed the getting acquainted period to receive enumerated medical treatment and services at Nao Medical without charge, up to $2,000 per calendar year, excluding lab fees and any other unusual expenses.

Immediate family members (spouse and children under 21 years of age) receive complimentary care on the covered services list, not including lab fees and any other unusual expenses. Employees should consult the covered services list for questions about available services and current pricing prior to requesting or obtaining services.

Employees are to clock out while receiving medical treatment from Nao Medical, and to schedule treatment for themselves and their families only at times designated by Nao Medical, or otherwise so as not to disrupt peak patient treatment hours. Treatment dollar amounts for employees and their families will not be included when calculating production numbers.

Nao Medical reserves the right at its sole discretion to refuse treatment to anyone. Employees are required to pay any balances due at time of service, unless special arrangements are approved at the discretion of Nao Medical, and accompanied by an authorization for deduction of up to 10% per paycheck. At the time of termination, employees will be required to arrange for payment of any outstanding balances due to Nao Medical.

Unemployment Insurance

Upon separation from employment, you may be entitled to state and federal unemployment compensation benefits. These laws generally provide eligible unemployed persons weekly cash benefits for a designated number of weeks or until such time as they are able to obtain employment. While we have no desire to deny unemployment benefits to any person who is legally entitled to them, it is Nao Medical's policy to contest all unemployment claims and apply for relief from charges for those claims that do not comply with the requirements of the law.
