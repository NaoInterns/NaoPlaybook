Resolving Patient Concerns

Sharad

Guides on managing reviews, people who are upset, etc.

Follow the good customer service principles based values

Specific issues / case scenarios

Multiple ways - process for how we manage

  1. On site - Patient complaint form - physical form they can fill in; raise concern to site manager
  1. Call and complain to call center - agent would fill out complaint form and send over; transfer call to supervisor
  1. Patient survey form - can rate from 1-5 star (1-3 is considered a complaint/concern - those are followed up on, unless patient asks not to be followed up with) - get a choice to post on Google, FB and Yelp - same process is followed, if negative reviews are received then we follow up with the same process.
  1. 4-5 stars is also followed up with to say thank you & post on Google reviews.
  1. Follow up on negative social media comments - standard response with an extension placed onto it for direct calls
  1. Complaints come via e-mails also (billing teams, customer service e-mails - any customer concerns)
  1. Follow up on most of the complaints except all of the ones which say don't contact further
  1. Internally, these get sent to the site managers for them to address it with the staff, when it's a staff-related complaint. Site managers address it with staff and go from there.
  1. Sent to Christine - physicians and nurse practitioners
  1. Dr. Zhang/Dr. Priti - doctors - Sharad sends out a note to provider asking for feedback or response - to understand their part of the concern is. Documented via an operational process in a separate Google sheet.
  1. Also get complaints about call center agents - tone, something said, misinformation, etc. When this happens, Sharad addresses it with the call center supervisors - call is pulled and addressed directly with the agent themselves. Depending on the situation, it's addressed - accordingly this is shared with the team.
  1. Very rarely - once or twice we get complaints via letter.
  1. Tracked and on a weekly basis, the team presents the data of the reviews to the executive leadership team and share it with the rest of the leadership team.
  1. Starting a process to include a site health check/employee health check - sending as an action plan to site managers; engaged week over week, month over month to see improvements.
  1. It would include customer service training - onboarding and after the fact.

The way we engage with patients - all patients get a call-back, e-mail, or text. Attempt to contact multiple times for negative reviews - for 4 stars+ we reach out to once. Leave voicemails with contact details on how to call back.

We listen to them, get their story and more details, pacify them - one call resolution - and send to management for feedback.

Every situation is different - takes different resolutions. Sometimes they just don't want to be heard; we apologize and say that we will be addressing the feedback with the appropriate site manager or provider.

If it's a medical issue, it might require Christine to call back - if there are concerns that Sharad can't call back on.

In very rare cases, we issue refunds - if the patient paid out of pocket any amount.

This is B2C customer. There are B2B customers as well with separate concerns and processes - same concept follows. Same process, situation to situation - sometimes result wasn't given, incomplete result, billing concerns, missing papers, employee was treated incorrectly, wasn't treated on time, etc. One to one management internally.
