  1. SERVICES NOT COVERED BY INSURANCE: The following services are not covered by insurance at our facility.
  • *THE ONLY EXCEPTION TO THE FOLLOWING IS IF A PATIENT HAS MEDICARE AS THEIR PRIMARY INSURANCE. IN THOSE CASES, WE SUBMIT EVERYTHING TO MEDICARE (EXCLUSIVE OF VACCINES).**
  1. Vaccines: We do not accept insurance for vaccines at our facility.
  1. Supplies: We do not accept insurance for supplies such as slings, crutches, air casts, knee immobilizers, etc. at our facility. Patients may use their insurance for their provider visit, but must pay out of pocket for any necessary supplies.
  1. CDL, TLC, & IMMIGRATION VISITS ARE NOT COVERED BY INSURANCE.
  1. Medical Records: We do not accept insurance for medical record requests. They are at an additional cost of $0.75/page.
  1. Forms: We do not accept insurance for the completion of forms that patients bring in. They are at an additional cost of $10/page.
  1. SPECIAL INSURANCE CASES: The following insurances have additional information that needs to be provided or verified in addition to the verification process on eClinicalWorks (detailed later on in this manual).
  1. MEDICARE: Patients with Medicare as their primary insurance should be made aware of the following:
  • Medicare holders have a start of year deductible which changes each January 1st. That deductible must be fulfilled before Medicare will start covering services rendered.
  • Medicare patients must complete a Medicare Advanced Beneficiary Notice at the start of each year. This notice specifies what Medicare does not cover & the patient must choose their preferred billing options on the form, followed by signature & date.
  • All claims must be submitted to Medicare by our facility, inclusive of those services that we do not normally accept insurance for.The ONLY exception is with regards to vaccinations. NO ADMINISTERED VACCINES ARE TO BE SUBMITTED TO MEDICARE EXCEPT THE TD (TETANUS) VACCINE IF NEEDED DUE TO INJURY (e.g.; stepped on a nail, dog bite, wound, etc).
  • Medicare patients will normally have a form of secondary insurance. These insurances must also be verified in addition to the primary Medicare insurance. Examples of secondary insurances are The Empire Plan, AARP, United Healthcare, Medicaid, etc.

b)    FIDELIS: There are three forms of Fidelis insurance; Fidelis Care of NY Exchange, Fidelis Medicaid, & Fidelis Medicare. ALL Fidelis insurances must be verified at the initial visit.

  • FIDELIS CARE OF NY EXCHANGE: These plans usually have a co-payment amount printed on the insurance card. It is best to call & verify the patient’s coverage. Even IF the member has a co-payment amount, a Fidelis Prior Authorization Form should be completed upon the first visit. If we receive a confirmation that states “No Prior Authorization Needed” from Fidelis, then the Prior Authorization form DOES NOT need to be completed for future visits.
  • If you speak to a representative at Fidelis, a Billing Alert should be added to the patient’s chart with the name of the person you spoke to at Fidelis, “no prior-auth needed,”& a timestamp. (e.g.; “Spoke to Bob at Fidelis. No prior-auth needed. <Vaishnav,Siddhi 03/16/2016 03:12:03 PM EDT >”)
  • FIDELIS MEDICAID: These plans usually need a Fidelis Prior Authorization Form to be completed before the patient is seen by the provider. It is best to call & verify the patient’s coverage. For Fidelis Medicaid patients, the Prior Authorization Form must be completed as follows:
  • The front desk must print:

1. Fidelis Care Member Name

2. Fidelis Care Member ID #

3. Patient’s Date of Birth

4. Patient’s Date of Procedure (the Date Of Service).

  • The scribe must print:

1. ICD-10 Diagnosis Code(s)

2. Services: CPT/Procedure Code(s) & Description.

  • The provider must bring the completed paperwork to the front desk by end of patient’s visit.
  • The front desk must fax the completed paperwork to Fidelis at the number printed on the Prior Authorization Form by end of day.
  • The front desk must scan both the completed paperwork & the fax confirmation into the patient’s chart in the “Ins Eligibility” folder as “Date/Prior Auth/Fax Conf Fidelis” (i.e.; “03.16.16 Prior Auth/Fax Conf Fidelis”).

1. This paperwork is NOT to be “Reviewed.”

2. You must select “Browse” in the “Description” section & choose the template for “FIDELIS PRIOR AUTH.”

3. Select “OK.”

4. Input the Date Of Service where it says “[DATE].”

5. Assign to “Billing, One.”

6. Select “OK.”

  • An approval or denial of the patient’s visit by Fidelis is usually faxed to our facility within 24 hours. The approval or denial must also be scanned into the patient’s chart in the “Ins Eligibility” folder as “Date/Approval or Denial Fidelis” (i.e.; “03.16.16 Approval Fidelis”, “03.16.16 Denial Fidelis”).

1. This paperwork is NOT to be “Reviewed.”

2. Assign to “Billing, One.”

3. Select “OK.”

  • If we receive a notice from Fidelis that states that a patient does not require prior authorization though they may have no specified co-payment on the card, you must add a Billing Alert stating “Patient does not require Fidelis Prior-Authorization”& a timestamp.   1. CO-PAYMENTS & DEDUCTIBLES:
  1. Statcare is not a Primary Care Provider (PCP); it is an Urgent Care Facility. Because of this, we collect either a “Specialist Co-Pay” or a specified “Urgent Care Co-Pay” from a patient. These are usually printed on the patient’s insurance card.
  1. If a patient has an in-network deductible, we collect $100.00 up front as payment which is applied to the patient’s deductible. Once a patient’s deductible has been met, only then will the co-payment apply. Patients with questions or hesitations with regards to this policy must call their insurance provider & clarify whether or not their in-network deductible applies to our specific facility & provide the name of the individual they spoke with/date of the call/reference #.
  • The $100.00 is collected to be applied towards the deductible for a patient’s initial visit.
  • For a follow-up visit for the SAME chief complaint, $75.00 is collected to be applied towards the deductible.
  • If the patient returns to the facility for a NEW chief complaint, $100.00 is collected once again, to be applied towards the deductible.
  1. MISCELLANEOUS:
  • ALWAYS check the address on the back of an insurance card to check the correct address for where to send MEDICAL CLAIMS. Different insurances with the same names (i.e.; EmblemHealth, United Healthcare, HIP, Fidelis, etc.) may sometimes have different addresses/P.O. Boxes depending on the patient’s specific plan. The incorrect address may delay any payment to our facility on behalf of the insurance company & may also affect a patient’s balance in our system.
  • ALWAYS scan the FRONT AND BACK of a patient’s insurance card into the chart.
  • ALWAYS verify the patient’s insurance using the “IE” button in the chart. If you receive an error message, you may call the insurance company and/or use the online portals available whose details are on the “Front Desk Master Spreadsheet” in your Google Drive.
  • ALWAYS verify that the insured’s name is input into their chart the exact way as is listed on their insurance card.
  • ALWAYS confirm who the primary holder of the insurance is. This will be input into the “Responsible Party” section in the patient’s chart (detailed later in the manual).
  • ALWAYS make sure that the name and face on the patient’s photo ID matches that of the name on the insurance card. If the patient is a minor (under the age of 18), verify that they are covered under the primary holder’s policy.
  • Medicaid Plans will have the patient as the “Responsible Party,” even if the patient is a minor. Some examples are United Healthcare Community Plan, Healthfirst (paper card), Fidelis Medicaid, Affinity Medicaid, etc.
  • If a patient asks for an itemized receipt for services rendered, you may do one of two things:
  1. Go to the Patient Hub & select “Medical Summary.” This will print out a sheet with procedure codes, dates of service, & any other pertinent information that the patient may need for their personal records or to submit to their insurance company.
  1. Ask a manager if they can access the patient’s payments through eClinicalWorks & print out a detailed & itemized receipt for the patient.
