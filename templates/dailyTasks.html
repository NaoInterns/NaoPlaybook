Daily Tasks

Each Morning:

Medical assistant, 9:30-10:00:

  • Check Nao Medical e-mail
  • Turn on lights
  • Candles
  • Turn air conditioning or heat on
  • Check refrigerator thermostat and fill out refrigerator log-in sheet
  • Be sure there are three pens in each room/drawer — turn on computers in each room!
  • Complete daily x-ray check sheet
  • Turn on the x-ray computer – make sure the VPN connection is made
  • X-ray lead coat to be hung in x-ray room/ x-ray ruler put in its proper place
  • Stock thermometer probes by vital sign machine
  • Clean/organize the office
  • Stock toilet paper in bathroom, urine cups, and paper towels in both bathrooms
  • Check for a tissue box and gloves in every room
  • Stock supplies in EACH drawer in the exam rooms and in the lab.
  • Make sure procedure room is fully stocked at all times.
  • Wipe down keyboards, phones, computers, exam tables, counter tops and door handles with Lysol wipes
  • Take the garbage to the dumpster (for Hicksville) if the cleaning people have not already done so

Front desk, 9:30-10:00:

  • Check Nao Medical e-mail
  • Turn off call forwarding
  • Check voicemail (516-804-4800, 4789#)
  • Make sure iPads are turned on and fully charged
  • Count petty cash - there should be $100 ($200 in Astoria)
  • Call to confirm all pending appts on the schedule for the day
  • Put all appts on schedule for provider on staff for that day
  • Appropriate staff members of issues with verifying insurances from the day
  • Stock candy, snacks
  • Scan all faxes from overnight and during the day in proper chart; labs, x-rays. The doctor review folder should never have anything in it from day to day

Throughout the day:

  • Call backs should be done everyday
  • PTA, CCA and web/text consent forms should be scanned into appropriate patient charts no matter what they are being seen for.
  • Lab test logs should be reconciled, do not let them pile up
  • Immigration paperwork should be up-to-date and all forms should be filled out by the MA who registered the patient. The majority of the forms should be completed the day the patient initially comes into the office.
  • Check to make sure all x-ray reports are printed and scanned into patient charts
  • Make sure all patients have follow up appointments
    • 1 week for blood work
    • 1-2 years for DoT
    • As told by provider

  • Switch off ALL computers
  • Shut down the x-ray computer, not just the monitor - shut it down completely
  • Garbage (ready based on your facility schedule)
  • Collect all Rx pads and put in doctors offices (Hicksille)
  • X-ray lead coat to be hung up.
  • Make sure all in-house are reviewed and results are recorded
